Introduction to Sammy Davis Jr.’s Legacy

The Height of Sammy Davis Jr.’s Career

Sammy Davis Jr. was a member of the Rat Pack, a group of entertainers that included Frank Sinatra, Dean Martin, Peter Lawford, and Joey Bishop. He was known for his versatility and was one of the most popular and highest-paid performers of his time.

Major Career Milestones

  • Debut on Broadway in “Mr. Wonderful” (1956)
  • Membership in the Rat Pack and performances in Las Vegas
  • Hit recordings such as “The Candy Man” and “What Kind of Fool Am I?”
  • Appearances in films like “Ocean’s 11” and “Robin and the 7 Hoods”

Financial Struggles Despite Success

Despite his success, Sammy Davis Jr. faced significant financial difficulties. His lavish lifestyle, poor financial management, and issues with the IRS contributed to his monetary struggles.

Lavish Lifestyle and Spending Habits

Sammy Davis Jr. was known for his extravagant spending. He lived a luxurious lifestyle that included expensive cars, jewelry, and clothing. His generosity towards friends and family also contributed to his financial drain.

Debts and Issues with the IRS

At the time of his death, Sammy Davis Jr. owed millions to the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) in back taxes. His failure to pay taxes on time compounded the problem, leading to a significant debt that he struggled to repay.

Income Sources and Earnings

Sammy Davis Jr.’s income came from various sources throughout his career. However, his earnings were not always managed well, which contributed to his financial issues.

Music and Recording Royalties

As a successful recording artist, Sammy Davis Jr. earned royalties from his music. However, these royalties were not enough to cover his expenses and debts.

Acting and Performance Fees

His acting roles in film, television, and theater, as well as his performance fees for live shows, were significant sources of income. Yet, these earnings were often offset by his extravagant spending.

Endorsements and Other Ventures

Sammy Davis Jr. also made money through endorsements and other business ventures. However, these were not always successful and did not significantly contribute to his net worth.

Failed Business Ventures

Endorsement Deals

Legal and Financial Challenges

Impact of Health on Finances

Inability to Perform
