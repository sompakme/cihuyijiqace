How do you ground a coaxial cable?

September 2024 · 4 minute read

Answer

One of the most straightforward methods of connecting your coaxial cable to the rest of your home is to route it via a grounding block and then run a wire from the block to a grounding electrode (ground rod) or other qualified grounding connection point.

Is it necessary to ground coax cable, and if so, how?

Under the National Electrical Code, the antenna mast and coaxial cable must be linked to the home’s ground electrode system (GES), which is often the ground rod situated near the electricity metre and circuit-breaker box. It is not necessary to use a separate ground rod, and it is not encouraged to do so.

What exactly is a coax grounding block in this context?

In a coaxial grounding block, static charges may be dissipated from the centre conductor without interfering with the transmission of the signal.
