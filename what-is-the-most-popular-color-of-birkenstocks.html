In German, what does Birkenstock mean?

Birkenstock Orthopädie GmbH & Co. KG is a German shoe company based in Neustadt (Wied). Birkenstocks, a German sandal brand, and other shoes with contoured cork and rubber footbeds (soles) that fit to the shape of their wearers’ feet are sold by the firm.

What’s the difference between standard Birkenstocks and soft bed Birkenstocks?

Birkenstock has two different types of foot beds. There’s the traditional (or standard) foot bed, and then there’s the soft foot bed. In terms of support, they are nearly identical; the only difference is that the soft foot bed contains a layer of padding between the layers of the foot bed, making it softer and more cushiony than the classic.

How do you tell whether your Birkenstocks are genuine?

On the sole of the shoe, look for the Birkenstock emblem with the registered trademark sign beside it. Just underneath the emblem, it will state “Made in Germany.” Make sure you double-check the spelling of the name because a fake sneaker can misspell it or not include it at all.
